Soil Biodiversity

Soil biodiversity refers to the variety of life forms found in the soil, including microorganisms, fungi, insects, and larger animals. These organisms play a critical role in ecosystem functions such as nutrient cycling, soil formation, and plant growth.

Importance of Soil Biodiversity

  • Enhances soil fertility
  • Improves water retention
  • Promotes plant health
  • Supports food webs
  • Helps in carbon storage

Threats to Soil Biodiversity

Soil biodiversity is threatened by factors such as:

  • Soil erosion
  • Pesticide use
  • Climate change
  • Urbanization
  • Monoculture farming

How to Protect Soil Biodiversity

To protect and promote soil biodiversity, consider the following practices:

  • Reduce chemical inputs
  • Practice crop rotation
  • Implement cover cropping
  • Support organic farming
  • Conserve natural habitats
